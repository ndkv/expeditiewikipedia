Crossing of the Snip

 

The direction of the voyage of the K18 at this point is a bit strange. After inspection of the navigation logbook of the captain, it became clear they stayed at one location for a complete day before they reached the ridge. This was strange because the location had no significant geophysical relevance (just another submarine volcanic landscape). The comments made by Vening Meinesz in his summaries of the voyage, made it more clear:

 

Leaving St. Vincent, our route brought us far out into the Atlantic. This loop was due to the crossing of a Dutch to the Netherlands West Indies; for providing this plane with the necessary radio-bearings for its position and for giving it indications about the weather, Hr. Ms. K18 had to be stationed for twenty-four hours at a certain location in the middle of the ocean.

 

Netherlands West Indies being Curacao. What aircraft? It was the KLM's maiden voyage to the West Indies. A Fokker F18, called the “Snip”, made his 8-day voyage from Amsterdam, Holland to Curacao. The promotion poster from that time shows the route of the Fokker F18:

It states "KLM Christmas Mail Flight to Dutch West-Indie, leaving from Schiphol on the 15th of December 1934". The Fokker F18 would eventually land in Curacao on the 22th of December. This would mean that it flew over the Hr. Ms. K18 around the 20th, which coincides with the track logbook. If you look closely to the poster, you can see a small (submarine-shape) figure in the middle of the Atlantic. Could this be the Hr. Ms. K18 with Vening Meinesz onboard? It is drawn slightly more the southwest, but still it is a beautiful detail.

 

The KLM Fokker F18 voyage would be the start of the famous inter-continental airline KLM. This is a nice example of two important voyages in Dutch history crossing paths, where Hr. Ms. K18 provided bearings and weather reports to the Snip.
